Welcome to a Bygone Era

Step back in time to the golden age of American road trips. Williams, Arizona is the last town on Historic **Route 66** to be bypassed by the interstate, preserving its classic charm and a slice of history. It is the perfect place to begin your grand adventure, as it's home to the one and only **Grand Canyon Railway**.

Hotels & Lodging in Williams

Whether you're looking for a cozy motel or a modern hotel, Williams, AZ has the perfect place to stay. Our **hotels** offer easy access to all the major attractions and are the perfect base for your **Grand Canyon** adventure. You can find everything from vintage Route 66-themed rooms to comfortable family-friendly lodging.

The Grand Canyon Railway & Polar Express +

Take a historic journey on a steam-powered train to the **Grand Canyon**. In winter, families can experience the magical **Polar Express** journey. The ride offers incredible views, onboard entertainment, and an authentic taste of travel from a different time.

Historic Route 66 +

Walk the famous "Mother Road" and explore vintage diners, classic motels, and nostalgic shops. The town's downtown area is a living museum, celebrating the spirit of the open road.

Williams Events & Car Shows +

Throughout the year, Williams hosts a variety of exciting **events**, from live music to the famous annual **car show**. These gatherings celebrate our history and community spirit, bringing the classic Route 66 culture to life.

Popular Nearby Sites

Your Williams adventure doesn't stop here. The town is the perfect launchpad for exploring some of Northern Arizona's most spectacular destinations.

Grand Canyon National Park +

A must-see natural wonder, the **Grand Canyon National Park** is a short drive or a scenic train ride away. From the South Rim, you can take in the breathtaking views, hike famous trails, and learn about the canyon's geology.

Bearizona Wildlife Park +

Just outside of Williams, **Bearizona** offers a unique opportunity to see North American wildlife from the comfort of your car. Drive through the park to see bears, wolves, and bison in a natural habitat, and then walk through the Fort Bearizona section for more animal encounters.

Sycamore Canyon +

For a serene and rugged adventure, explore the spectacular Sycamore Canyon. This area is a hidden gem for hiking, bird watching, and experiencing the untouched beauty of the Arizona wilderness.

Kaibab Lake +

Just a few miles north, **Kaibab Lake** is a tranquil escape for fishing and camping. Enjoy a peaceful day on the water, surrounded by the beautiful ponderosa pine forest.
